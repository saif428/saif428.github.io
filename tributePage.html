THANK YOU #9

"For every high school kid out there or college player, there is greatness in being the kind of teammate who truly wants to be part of a team. Everyone wants to be the reason they winning or losing. Every single one of us wants to be that person. But there are special moments that come from a shared commitment to play a role, while doing it together. That's what you will remember. Not your stats or your prestige, but the relationships and the achievement that you created through a group. It's hard to do, but there's great joy in that."

-Tony Romo

CAREER TIMELINE

  • 2003 --- Entered 2003 NFL Draft, but went undrafted and was signed by the Dallas Cowboys as an undrafted free agent.

  • 2006 --- Earns the starting quarterback position for the Dallas Cowboys.

  • 2007 --- First Pro Bowl apperance in 2007.

  • 2007 --- Signs a six year sixty-seven million dollar contract with the Dallas Cowboys.

  • 2009 --- Leads the Dallas Cowboys to be the NFC East Division Champions and earns his first playoff win of his career.

  • 2010 --- During the season, Tony Romo goes down with a broken left clavicle and was out for the majority of the season.

  • 2012 --- Surpasses Troy Aikman on the all-time passing touchdown leader for the Dallas Cowboys.

  • 2013 --- Signs a six year one hundred and eight million dollar extension with Dallas, but goes down in Week 16 with a herniated disk.

  • 2014 --- Led the league in completion percentage, and quarterback rating, but loses in the divisional playoff round against the Packers after a controversial call.

  • 2015 --- Goes down with a broken collarbone Week 2, comes back to play Week 12, but reinjures his collarbone forcing him to end his season.

  • 2016 --- During the preseason, Tony suffers compression fracture in his back causing him to miss 10 weeks.

  • 2016 --- After the great success of the rookie Dak Prescott, Romo would serve as the backup during this season.

  • 2016 --- Tony Romo comes in for one drive and throws his final touchdown of his career.

  • 2017 --- After a long career as a Cowboy, Tony Romo announces his retirement from the NFL.
